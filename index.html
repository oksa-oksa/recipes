Eggs muffins

Prep Time: 10 min

Cook Time: 20 min

Serves: 4

Egg muffins are one of the best healthy breakfasts for meal prepping because you easily make a big batch of them at once and they will keep in your fridge for up to a week.

Ingredients

  • 7 large eggs
  • ½ zucchini
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 red pepper
  • 5-6 champignons
  • 70-100 g cheese
  • salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the vegetables. Choop all the vegetables into small cubes and mix them in a bowl.
  2. Take metal or silicone molds and fill them with vegetables about halfway.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk eggs, salt, pepper and cheese.
  4. Pour mixture into prepared muffin tin.
  5. Bake egg muffins in the oven at 180 degrees until they increase in volume. It's about 15-20 minutes. Then give some time to cool and then remove from the molds.

Avocado toast with egg

Prep Time: 5 min

Cook Time: 15 min

Serves: 1

This is a simple healthy protein-packed breakfast, snack or light meal! Simple to make, tasty and satisfying. If you haven’t had an egg and avocado toast breakfast yet, now is the time!

Ingredients

  • 1 slice of bread, toasted
  • ½ ripe medium avocado
  • salt and pepper, to taste
  • olive oil, to taste

Steps

  1. Use a fork to smash the avocado down onto the toasted bread slice. Scatter a small squeeze of lemon juice over the avocado.
  2. Heat some butter (or olive oil). Break an egg onto the skillet and reduce the heat to low. Cook it uncovered until the whites set. You could cover it up once the whites set if you wanted a more firm yolk.
  3. Place the egg on top of the smashed avocado.
  4. Finish by sprinkling a little pepper, salt, and a very light drizzle of olive oil on top of the egg.

Omlet with spinach

Prep Time: 5-7 min

Cook Time: 7-10 min

Serves: 2

You can add vegetables, greens, cheese, meat or sausage to beaten eggs. A wonderful summer breakfast is "green" omelet - with spinach, green onions and green peas.

Ingredients

  • 200 g spinach
  • 3-4 eggs
  • 4 feathers green onion
  • 50-70 g Feta cheese
  • 40 g Green peas
  • salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Beat eggs in a bowl with salt and pepper. Grate the cheese on a large grater.
  2. Boil the peas for three minutes in salted and sweetened boiling water.
  3. Melt the butter in a frying pan and fry half the spinach on it for three minutes, stirring constantly.
  4. Add finely chopped green onions, fry for another minute
  5. Add peas and pour beaten eggs. Sprinkle with cheese and send for 10 minutes in a preheated oven to 180 degrees. When serving, lay out the other half of the spinach leaves and fold the omelette in half.

Mushrooms with smoked salmon and poached egg

Prep Time: 15 min

Cook Time: 5-7 min

Serves: 1

Fried mushrooms with poached egg is another amazing recipe in your breakfast treasury.

Ingredients

  • 300 g champignons
  • 1 large egg
  • 30 g smoked salmon
  • salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Wash the mushrooms, cut them into large pieces and fry in butter with salt and pepper until the mushrooms are soft.

Caprese croissant

Prep Time: 10 min

Cook Time: 5 min

Serves: 2-4

The perfect breakfast or just snack. Simple to make, tasty and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 4–6 croissants, sliced in half, lengthwise
  • 8 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 250 g mozzarella (2 mozzarella balls)
  • 4 tsp basil pesto
  • arugula salad for serving
  • Basil leaves for serving

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Arrange bottom half of croissants in a single layer on a large baking sheet, lined with parchment paper.
  2. Spread the pesto sauce over each half, then stuff with sun-dried tomatoes, artichoke hearts, cherry tomatoes, mozzarella and arugula leaves into the croissant. Top with second half of the croissant buns.
  3. Brush each croissants with melted butter, sprinkle with black sesame, and bake for about 5 minutes or until croissants are crispy and cheese melted. If at anytime, the top is browning, cover with aluminium foil during baking. Serve immediately with a few fresh basil leaves if desired.

Ham and cheese sandwich

Prep Time: 20 min

Cook Time: 25 min

Serves: 4

Boozy, buttery, cheesy French toast is made for a lazy weekend breakfast.

Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • ½ cup of milk
  • pinch of kosher salt
  • Dijon mustard
  • 12 ham slices
  • 100 g cheese

Steps

  1. Make the French toast: In a medium, shallow bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, sugar, salt and nutmeg.
  2. In a nonstick skillet set over medium heat, melt enough clarified butter to coat the bottom of the pan. Dip a slice of brioche into the egg mixture, and let rest for about 15 seconds on each side before transferring to the hot pan. Cook until golden brown on both sides, about 2 minutes per side. Transfer to a wire rack, then repeat with remaining bread slices.
  3. Spread each slice of warm French toast with as much Dijon mustard as you’d like. Top each piece with three slices ham and sprinkle with cheese. Place on a baking sheet.
  4. Broil for about 2 minutes, until ham is toasted and cheese is bubbling and melted. (Keep an eye on the sandwiches; they burn quickly.) Serve warm.

Suluguni tortilla wraps

Prep Time: 10 min

Cook Time: 15 min

Serves: 4

This is a good way to diversify your breakfast routine.

Ingredients

  • 1 garlic clove, crushed
  • 125 ml Greek yogurt
  • 225g suluguni cheese
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 4 large soft tortilla wraps
  • 1 Little Gem lettuce, shredded
  • 150g cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cucumber, halved, seeded and sliced
  • ½ small red onion, thinly sliced
  • a handful of pitted olives, sliced

Steps

  1. Mix the garlic and yogurt together with some salt and pepper.
  2. Slice the suluguni cheese into 8 pieces. Brush with oil on both sides, sprinkle with the oregano.
  3. Sear the suluguni for about 2 minutes each side until marks appear. Warm the tortilla wraps briefly to soften them.
  4. Divide the garlic yogurt between the wraps, then add some lettuce and 2 slices of suluguni to each one. Top with cherry tomatoes, cucumber, onion and olives. Bring the bottom up and then fold the sides in tightly. Serve immediately.

Banana and Nutella French Toast

Cook Time: 5 min

Serves: 2

Make soft French toast, spread them with the coveted Nutella, lay out a banana and forget about everything. On Sunday morning you can do everything, especially happiness for breakfast.

  • 4 pieces of bread

Banana pancakes

Prep Time: 5 min

Cook Time: 15 min

Serves: 4

Packed with bananas, light and fluffy – this banana pancake recipe is super easy to make, any time of day!

Ingredients

  • k1 cup of milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbs butter
  • 1½ cups all purpose flour
  • 3 tbs dark brown sugar
  • 1 tbs baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 small bananas
  • mini chocolate chips for serving
  • maple syrup for serving

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the wet ingredients.
  2. In a larger bowl, combine the dry ingredients.
  3. Mash 2 small bananas.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry and add the mashed bananas. Fold together gently until just combined.
  5. Heat 1 Tbs butter in a skillet over medium heat.
  6. Add pancake batter ½ to ¾ per pancake
  7. Cook until bubbles form and pop, then flip. Cook for another minute or two, until golden brown.
  8. Add bananas, syrup and chocolate chips to your pancake stack.

Caesar salad

Prep Time: 15 min

Cook Time: 10-15 min

Serves: 2

A classic chicken salad recipe, featuring crunchy croutons and a creamy, garlic dressing. Ideal for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 8 slices baguette, cut into croutons
  • 200g cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 large head romaine lettuce
  • 25g grated Parmesan
  • 3 skinless chicken breasts, char-grilled and thinly sliced
  • 120ml readymade mayonnaise
  • ½ garlic clove, crushed
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • ½ lemon, juiced

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 170 degrees. Put the croutons on one baking sheet and the tomatoes on another. Drizzle both with a little olive oil. Season both well and put in the oven. Bake the croutons for 10 minutes and the tomatoes for 15-20 minutes, then set both aside.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the ripped lettuce, Parmesan and croutons and set aside. Put the dressing ingredients in a bowl, stir to combine. Taste and add a little more lemon juice, as required.
  3. Toss the dressing with the lettuce and crouton mixture just before you are ready to serve. Put in a bowl and top with the roasted tomatoes and cooked chicken.
  4. Grate parmesan over the top.

Shrimp salad

Prep Time: 15 min

Cook Time: 5-7 min

Serves: 2

Avocado shrimp salad topped with homemade citrus miso dressing is what summer is all about! This salad is loaded with peppery arugula, tons of avocado slices, juicy mandarin oranges, and garlic butter sauteed shrimp!

Ingredients

  • 225g raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 large avocado, diced
  • handful cherry tomatoes, diced
  • ½ red onion, minced
  • 40g Feta cheese
  • 2 tbsp salted butter, melted
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Toss shrimp with melted butter in a bowl until well-coated.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Add shrimp to the skillet in a single layer, searing for a minute or until it starts to become pink around the edges, then flip and cook until shrimp are cooked through, less than a minute.
  3. Transfer shrimp to a shallow plate and allow cooling while you prepare the other ingredients.
  4. Add all other ingredients (avocado, tomato, red onion, and cilantro) to a large mixing bowl. drizzle with olive oil and lime juice and toss to mix everything together.
  5. Add cooked shrimp and give a quick stir to mix together. Season the shrimp avocado salad with more salt and pepper, to taste.

Salad with grilled pepper

Prep Time: 10 min

Cook Time: 15 min

Serves: 4

This salad is perfect lunch for summer days.

Ingredients

  • 2 red peppers
  • 50g Feta cheese
  • 400g ripe cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 30g fresh basil

Steps

  1. Place the peppers directly onto a naked gas flame or under a very hot grill and let them blacken and blister. Turn now and then with a pair of metal kitchen tongs. When blackened all over, pop them into a bowl and cover with clingfilm.
  2. Leave the peppers to steam for about 10 minutes, then remove the clingfilm and leave them to cool.
  3. When they are cool enough to handle, rub off the blackened skin and brush away the seeds from the inside.
  4. Cut the feta cheese and tear the peppers into strips and divide over the plates of mozzarella. Halve the tomatoes and scatter over the top.
  5. Season well with sea salt and black pepper and drizzle with olive oil. Pick and tear up the basil leaves and sprinkle them over the salad before serving.

Turkey with cranberry sauce

Prep Time: 10 min

Cook Time: 15 min

Serves: 2

This turkey recipe couldn’t be any easier to prepare for a quick and easy dinner idea and this will become your new favourite turkey recipe - divine and tender meat with dried fruit and cranberry sauce!

Ingredients

  • 700-800g turkey breast
  • 10ml white wine, to taste
  • 15-20g dried apricots
  • cinnamon, to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • salt and pepper, to taste
  • 200g cranberry
  • honey, to taste
  • sugar, to taste

Steps

  1. Finely chop dried apricots and dried figs (dried fruit should be so much that they can abundantly cover the turkey breast).
  2. Mix dried fruits with cinnamon, salt, white wine, black pepper, barberry and olive oil.
  3. Rub our mixture on the meat. It should be densely covered with dried fruits and spices.

As soon as the cranberries heat up - add sugar and honey. Evaporate over low heat until the sauce thickens. Serve the turkey with cranberry sauce.

Baked veal

Cook Time: 10-15 min

Serves: 2

Marinate thin grilled veal chops in lemon juice and cooking with lots of bay leaves. It gives the meat a completely unique aroma and taste. Just 15 minutes and the amazingly fragrant veal will be ready.

Ingredients

  • 400g veal

  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 10 pcs bay leaf
  • salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Cut the meat into thin slices and beat with a cooking hammer. Add salt and pepper, sprinkle with lemon juice and add bay leaves. Leave for 3 minutes. Fresh leaves are ideal for cooking this dish, but you can do with dry ones.

Smoky salmon

Prep Time: 7 min

Cook Time: 15-20 min

Serves: 4

This Grilled Salmon is a little sweet and a little savory marinated with soy sauce, brown sugar, and herbs.

Ingredients

  • 500-600g salmon fillets
  • 25g brown sugar
  • 20-25ml olive oil
  • 25-30ml soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 1 tsp garlic powder

Steps

  1. Whisk together all the ingredients except for the fish in a bowl, and pour into a resealable plastic bag. Add the salmon fillets, turning to coat with the marinade, squeeze out excess air, and seal the bag. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, turning occasionally.
  2. reheat an outdoor grill for medium heat, and lightly oil the grate. Remove the salmon from the marinade, and shake off excess. Discard the remaining marinade.
  3. Grill the salmon on the preheated grill until browned and the fish flakes easily with a fork, about 4-5 minutes on each side. This can also be cooked in a 180-200 degree oven for 8-10 minutes depending on the thickness of your salmon.

Oreo parfait

Prep Time: 10 min

Cook Time: 15 min

Serves: 10-12

This dessert made special! Make this classic chocolatey deliciousness into individual servings, for an easy dessert!

Ingredients

  • 36 oreo cookies
  • 170g chocolate instant pudding mix
  • 2 cups milk
  • 50-60g butter
  • 450g whipped topping
  • 250g mascarpone
  • 1 cup powdered sugar

Steps

  1. Mix the pudding mix with the milk. Set aside in the refrigerator to set up.
  2. Crush the oreos.
  3. Mix the remaining cookie crumbs with the melted butter, and divide among 10 jars, glasses or cups.
  4. Beat half the whipped topping, cream cheese and powdered sugar together until fully blended.
  5. Dollop on top of the cookie crumbs in each glass and smooth a little.
  6. Top with chocolate pudding and smooth a little.
  7. Add the remaining whipped topping to each glass, and then sprinkle the remaining cookie crumbs over the top.
  8. Refrigerate until dessert time.
